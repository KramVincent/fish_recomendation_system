# FishDiet - User Guide & System Explanation

## What Is This Application?

**FishDiet** is a smart health assistant app that helps people with chronic health conditions make safe decisions about eating fish. Think of it as your personal fish nutritionist that knows your health situation and tells you which fish are safe to eat, which ones to be careful with, and which ones to avoid.

---

## Who Is This App For?

- **People with chronic illnesses** (diabetes, high blood pressure, kidney disease, etc.)
- **Healthcare professionals/Dietitians** who want to provide fish recommendations to their patients
- **Family members or caregivers** managing meals for someone with health conditions
- **Anyone trying to eat fish safely** based on their personal health needs

---

## Main Features (What Can You Do?)

### 1. Fish Photo Check ("Is This Fish Safe For Me?")

**What it does:** Take a picture of any fish (at the market, restaurant, or home) and the app will:
- Identify what type of fish it is
- Tell you if it's safe for YOU based on your health conditions
- Give you portion size recommendations
- Suggest how often you can eat it
- Warn you of any concerns specific to your conditions

**Example:** 
> Maria has diabetes and high blood pressure. She uploads a photo of sardines. The app identifies it and tells her: "Avoid - Sardines are commonly sold canned or salted with high sodium content, which is not good for your blood pressure."

### 2. Health Profile Management

**What it does:** Store and manage health information for yourself or others (patients, family members).

**You can save:**
- **Health conditions** (diabetes, hypertension, kidney disease, gout, asthma, etc.)
- **Age, weight, and height**
- **Allergies**
- **Medications**
- **Dietary preferences** (low-sodium, halal, kosher, etc.)
- **Pregnancy status** (important for mercury recommendations)
- **Personal notes**

**Why multiple profiles?**
- A dietitian can manage recommendations for many patients
- A parent can check fish safety for different family members
- You can keep separate profiles for different health scenarios

### 3. Personalized Fish Recommendations

**What it does:** Shows you a complete guide of all fish types, color-coded by safety for your specific health conditions:

| Color | Meaning | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| 🟢 **Green/Safe** | This fish is beneficial for your conditions | Eat freely with recommended portions |
| 🟡 **Yellow/Moderate** | This fish requires caution | Eat occasionally with smaller portions |
| 🔴 **Red/Avoid** | This fish may worsen your conditions | Do not eat or strictly limit |

**For each fish, you get:**
- Recommended portion size (e.g., "100-120g")
- How often you can eat it (e.g., "2-3 times per week")
- Medical reasoning explaining why
- Alternative fish suggestions if this one isn't safe
- Cooking/preparation tips

### 4. Dashboard & Statistics

**What it does:** Your personal homepage showing:
- How many fish are safe for you vs. moderate vs. avoid
- Your recent fish checks (photos you've uploaded)
- Quick access to all features
- Your current health conditions at a glance

## The Smart System Behind It

### How Does It Know What's Safe?

The app uses a **medical knowledge database** created by nutrition and health experts. Here's how it works:

#### Step 1: Fish Nutritional Data
Every fish in the database has detailed nutritional information:
- **Protein content** - How much protein per serving
- **Omega-3 fatty acids** - Heart-healthy fats
- **Mercury level** - Can be harmful in large amounts
- **Sodium content** - Important for blood pressure
- **Phosphorus & Potassium** - Critical for kidney disease patients
- **Vitamins** (D, B12, etc.)
- **Calories and fat content**

#### Step 2: Health Condition Rules
For each health condition, experts have defined rules:

| Condition | What to Watch | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| **Diabetes** | Fat content, glycemic impact | High-fat fish can affect blood sugar control |
| **Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)** | Sodium/salt content | Salt raises blood pressure |
| **Kidney Disease (CKD)** | Phosphorus, potassium, protein | Damaged kidneys can't filter these well |
| **Gout** | Purine content | Purines increase uric acid, causing gout attacks |
| **Heart Disease** | Omega-3, cholesterol | Omega-3 helps the heart; cholesterol can harm it |
| **Asthma/COPD** | Omega-3 anti-inflammatory | Omega-3 may reduce airway inflammation |

#### Step 3: Single vs. Multiple Conditions
The system is extra smart about **multiple conditions** (multimorbidity):

**Example:** If you have BOTH diabetes AND hypertension:
- The system doesn't just combine two separate recommendations
- It uses special rules that consider how both conditions interact together
- It picks the safest recommendation that works for ALL your conditions

This is important because:
> A fish might be great for diabetes but terrible for hypertension. The system makes sure you only get fish that are safe for ALL your conditions at once.

## Understanding the Recommendations

### Safety Levels Explained

**🟢 SUITABLE / SAFE**
> "This fish fits well with your health needs"
- Eat 2-3 times per week
- Normal portion sizes (100-120g)
- No significant concerns for your conditions

**🟡 MODERATE / CAUTION**
> "This fish is okay but be careful"
- Limit to 1-2 times per week
- Smaller portions (80-100g)
- Some ingredients may slightly affect your condition
- Consider alternatives when possible

**🔴 AVOID**
> "This fish may harm your health"
- Do not eat, or eat very rarely
- If you must eat it, use tiny portions
- Ask your doctor before consuming
- Use the suggested alternatives instead

### What Information You Get Per Fish

1. **Portion Size**: How much to eat (in grams) per serving
2. **Frequency**: How often you can eat it (per week or month)
3. **Clinical Rationale**: Medical explanation in simple terms
4. **Alternatives**: Other fish that are safer for you with similar taste
5. **Preparation Tips**: How to cook it to maximize safety (e.g., grilled vs. fried, fresh vs. canned)
6. **Warnings**: Specific things to watch for

## Understanding Mercury in Fish

### What is Mercury?
Mercury is a naturally occurring metal that accumulates in fish. High levels can be harmful, especially for:
- Pregnant women
- Breastfeeding mothers  
- Young children
- People eating large amounts of fish

### Mercury Levels in the App

| Level | Meaning | Fish Examples |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| **Very Low** | Safest choice | Tilapia, Milkfish, Sardines |
| **Low** | Generally safe | Mackerel |
| **Moderate** | Limit consumption | Some tuna species |
| **High** | Avoid or rarely eat | Larger predator fish |
| **Very High** | Avoid completely | Shark, Swordfish |

## Technical Notes (For Understanding, Not Required)

### How the AI Identifies Fish
When you upload a photo, the app uses image recognition technology to:
1. Analyze the fish's appearance (shape, color, fins, etc.)
2. Match it against a database of known fish species
3. Return the most likely matches with confidence scores

### How Recommendations Are Calculated
1. Your health conditions are loaded from your profile
2. The system looks up each fish's nutritional data
3. It checks the "suitability rules" for each condition you have
4. For multiple conditions, it uses special combined rules
5. The most restrictive (safest) recommendation is shown

| Term | Meaning |

| **Omega-3** | Healthy fats that help your heart and reduce inflammation |

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is this app a replacement for my doctor?**
> No. This app is a helpful guide, but always consult your healthcare provider for medical decisions.

**Q: How accurate is the fish identification?**
> The AI provides confidence scores with each identification. For important decisions, verify with the fish seller or take a clear photo.

**Q: Can I use this for other seafood?**
> Currently, the app focuses on common fish species. Shellfish and other seafood may be added in future updates.

**Q: What if my fish isn't in the database?**
> The app will try to identify it based on similarity. If it's not recognized, you can manually look up similar fish in the recommendations.

**Q: Can I share my recommendations with my doctor?**
> Yes! A future feature will allow you to download PDF reports to share with healthcare providers.
